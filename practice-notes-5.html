The one method — works for every case on the sheet

Five steps (Prof. da Silva's algorithm). The same recipe solves all 27 sub-cases — only the arithmetic changes.

  1. Name the nodes A, B, C…
  2. Check it is isostatic — reactions + internal-hinge equations must equal the unknowns.
  3. Find the reactions from global equilibrium — plus M = 0 at every internal hinge for the extra equation(s).
  4. Pick a side (left or right) and cut at a general distance x inside each segment.
  5. Equilibrium of the cut piece → N(x), V(x), M(x). A new segment begins at every discontinuity: support, point load, applied couple, start/end of a distributed load, corner, or hinge.

The dashed line marks the reference bottom fibre; for a column it rotates with the bar. N = 0 whenever nothing acts along the bar's own axis — true for every beam a–t.

Two golden rules: dM/dx = V → M is extreme exactly where V = 0; and M = 0 at free ends, plain pin/roller ends, and internal hinges.
