Problem 3.1 — Idealized structural systems

For each of the structural beams in Fig. 3.1, the idealized structural model must reproduce the geometry (span, height) and boundary conditions (which displacements and/or rotations the supports prevent).

Supports recap:
  • Roller (carrello) — blocks 1 translation (the one ⊥ to the rolling surface). 2 d.o.f. free.
  • Pin / hinge (cerniera) — blocks 2 translations (H & V). Rotation is free.
  • Fixed / clamp (incastro) — blocks 2 translations AND rotation. 0 d.o.f. free.
(a) Beam resting on two masonry supports (L = 10 m)
Both masonry supports prevent vertical settlement. Horizontal sliding is essentially free on at least one side (otherwise the beam couldn't be placed). The standard idealization is therefore pin + roller (simply supported beam) — 3 reactions, statically determinate.
(b) Beam embedded in a wall (L = 5 m)
The wall fully constrains the end of the beam: no translation (H, V) and no rotation. ⇒ fixed support (encastre). Free end. 3 reactions: VA, HA, MA.
(c) Lintel over a wall opening — no wall above (L = 6 m)
Both wall piers support the lintel from below; the ends of the lintel can rotate freely (no continuous wall above to clamp them). Idealization: pin + roller (simply supported beam), 3 reactions, statically determinate.
(d) Lintel embedded in a continuous wall — wall extends above (L = 6 m)
The wall is continuous above the lintel (it doesn't break at the opening). The masonry mass above clamps the lintel's ends, preventing both rotation and translation. Idealization: fixed–fixed beam (incastro on both sides), 6 reactions (V, H, M at each end) ⇒ 3 times hyperstatic.

Problem 3.2 — Reactions of the supports

Procedure for every case: (1) draw FBD with reactions, (2) write the 3 equilibrium equations (ΣFx, ΣFy, ΣMpt), (3) solve. Sign convention: forces ↑ and → positive; moments counter‑clockwise (CCW) positive.

(a) Simply supported beam · P = 7 kN at midspan · L = 10 m

FBD & Equilibrium

Σ Fx = 0: HA = 0 Σ MA = 0 (CCW⁺): VB·(10) − 7·(5) = 0 ⇒ VB = 35 / 10 = 3.5 kN ↑ Σ Fy = 0: VA + VB − 7 = 0 ⇒ VA = 7 − 3.5 = 3.5 kN ↑
VA = 3.5 kN (↑)   HA = 0   VB = 3.5 kN (↑)
Symmetric loading on a symmetric structure ⇒ symmetric reactions. Quick check ✓
(b) Simply supported beam · P = 10 kN at 2.5 m from A · L = 10 m

FBD & Equilibrium

Σ Fx = 0: HA = 0 Σ MA = 0: VB·(10) − 10·(2.5) = 0 ⇒ VB = 25 / 10 = 2.5 kN ↑ Σ Fy = 0: VA + VB − 10 = 0 ⇒ VA = 10 − 2.5 = 7.5 kN ↑
VA = 7.5 kN (↑)   HA = 0   VB = 2.5 kN (↑)
The load is closer to A, so A carries more — as expected from lever‑arm intuition.
(c) Beam with concentrated couple 10 kN·m beyond support B · A pin, B roller

FBD & Equilibrium

Σ Fx = 0: HA = 0 Σ MA = 0 (CCW⁺): VB·(7) − 10 = 0 ← couple is CW (negative) ⇒ VB = 10 / 7 ≈ 1.43 kN ↑ Σ Fy = 0: VA + VB = 0 ⇒ VA = − 1.43 kN = 1.43 kN ↓
VA = 1.43 kN (↓)   HA = 0   VB = 1.43 kN (↑)
Key idea: a pure couple does not translate but rotates. Equilibrium requires the two reactions to form an equal and opposite couple. The arm is 7 m (distance A–B), magnitude = 10 / 7. The direction of each reaction follows from balancing the applied moment.
(d) Cantilever fixed at A · two 10 kN ↓ loads · 5 kN ← at the tip · L = 5 m

Given structure

Free-body diagram

applied load support reaction

Step-by-step solution

  1. Count the reactions. A clamp (fixed support) blocks horizontal translation, vertical translation and rotation ⇒ 3 unknowns: HA, VA, MA. The tip is free, so no reactions there.
  2. Horizontal equilibrium  Σ Fx = 0  (→ positive). The only horizontal force is the 5 kN pulling left:
    HA − 5 = 0 ⇒ HA = 5 kN (→)
  3. Vertical equilibrium  Σ Fy = 0  (↑ positive). Both 10 kN loads point down:
    VA − 10 − 10 = 0 ⇒ VA = 20 kN (↑)
  4. Moment about A  Σ MA = 0  (CCW positive). Summing moments at A makes HA and VA drop out (zero arm). Each downward load sits to the right of A, turning the beam clockwise (negative), so the clamp resists counter‑clockwise:
    MA − 10·(3) − 10·(5) = 0 ⇒ MA = 80 kN·m (↺)
  5. Why the 5 kN drops out of the moment. Its line of action runs along the beam axis, straight through A, so its lever arm about A is zero. A force makes a moment only when it is offset from the point.
VA = 20 kN (↑)    HA = 5 kN (→)    MA = 80 kN·m (↺)
Sanity check: the beam is held at one end only, so the clamp alone must carry every applied force (20 kN up, 5 kN across) and resist the full overturning moment (80 kN·m). ✓
(e) Simply supported beam · UDL q = 5 kN/m over the full span · L = 10 m

Given structure

FBD — distributed load → its resultant

applied load resultant of UDL support reaction

Step-by-step solution

  1. Replace the distributed load. A uniform load over a length is equivalent to a single resultant at its centroid (the middle of the rectangle):
    R = q · L = 5 · 10 = 50 kN, acting at x = L/2 = 5 m from A
  2. Count the reactions. A = roller ⇒ one vertical reaction VA; C = pin ⇒ VC + HC. 3 unknowns, 3 equations ⇒ determinate.
  3. Horizontal equilibrium  Σ Fx = 0. There is no horizontal applied load:
    HC = 0
  4. Moment about A  Σ MA = 0  (CCW positive). Only R and VC have a lever arm about A:
    VC·(10) − 50·(5) = 0 ⇒ VC = 250 / 10 = 25 kN (↑)
  5. Vertical equilibrium  Σ Fy = 0:
    VA + VC − 50 = 0 ⇒ VA = 25 kN (↑)
VA = 25 kN (↑)    VC = 25 kN (↑)    HC = 0
Symmetry check: a symmetric load on a symmetric beam splits equally — each support carries qL/2 = 25 kN. ✓
(f) Simply supported beam · UDL q = 10 kN/m on the left half (0 → 5 m) · L = 10 m

Given structure

FBD — distributed load → its resultant

applied load resultant of UDL support reaction

Step-by-step solution

  1. Replace the distributed load. The UDL acts only on the left 5 m. Its resultant equals the area of the load rectangle, placed at that rectangle's centroid:
    R = q · a = 10 · 5 = 50 kN, acting at x = a/2 = 2.5 m from A
  2. Count the reactions. A = roller ⇒ VA; C = pin ⇒ VC + HC. 3 unknowns ⇒ determinate.
  3. Horizontal equilibrium  Σ Fx = 0:
    HC = 0
  4. Moment about A  Σ MA = 0  (CCW positive). The resultant sits 2.5 m from A, VC sits 10 m from A:
    VC·(10) − 50·(2.5) = 0 ⇒ VC = 125 / 10 = 12.5 kN (↑)
  5. Vertical equilibrium  Σ Fy = 0:
    VA + VC − 50 = 0 ⇒ VA = 37.5 kN (↑)
VA = 37.5 kN (↑)    VC = 12.5 kN (↑)    HC = 0
Intuition: the load sits closer to A, so A carries the larger share. The resultant is 2.5 m from A and 7.5 m from C ⇒ the reactions split in the inverse ratio 7.5 : 2.5 = 3 : 1, i.e. 37.5 vs 12.5 kN. ✓

Common mistakes to avoid:
  1. Forgetting to set H = 0 when no horizontal load exists — still write the equation explicitly.
  2. Mixing up signs for the applied couple (CW vs CCW). Pick a convention (CCW⁺) and stick with it.
  3. Putting the resultant of a UDL at the wrong location — always at the centroid of the load shape (mid‑span for a rectangle, L/3 from the larger end for a triangle).
  4. Computing MA for a cantilever using forces only on one side and forgetting the reaction moment.
